Keep the owner-led feel.

The current page wins because it feels like a real local specialist, not a franchise. The redesign should protect that while making the page commercially sharper.

What gets fixed first

The estimate path arrives earlier, the first interaction asks for less, and the contact section feels deliberate instead of appended after the long-scroll story.

Premium estimate rail

What definitely stays

Blue/yellow identity, bold contractor tone, founder visibility, and the premium-home positioning that makes this business feel distinct.

This needs tightening, not flattening.

  • The contact moment comes too late. Visitors who already believe the quality story should not have to reach the lower half of the page before they can raise a hand.
  • The first-step form asks for too much. Preferences, timing, and extra detail can come later. The first move should be faster.
  • The premium pitch is under-staged. The copy says “distinctive Spokane homes,” but the current layout does not fully deliver on that level of design confidence.

Design direction

“Make it feel like the boutique contractor people call for the expensive project, not the generic local site they browse and forget.”

That means stronger hierarchy, more deliberate color blocking, and a page structure that sells premium work sooner instead of explaining everything first and asking later.

Hero rebuild

  • Primary CTA A harder-working “Request a fast estimate” action replaces the softer consultation rhythm.
  • Trust cluster License, service footprint, and premium scope sit right beside the ask.
  • Short form Name, preferred contact, job type, and short details only.

Service routing

  • Luxury remodel lane Built for kitchen, bath, and custom fixture projects.
  • New construction lane Signals design and install confidence for higher-value residential work.
  • Hot water lane Lets upgrade traffic convert without being buried inside the broader story.

Production handoff

  • Static build Faster mobile experience with stronger visual hierarchy.
  • Copy cleanup Sharper headline system and a cleaner contact section.
  • Launch-ready Designed so it can be moved into a real small-business stack without friction.
