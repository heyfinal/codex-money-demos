Make the estimate path clearer and lighter.

This concept keeps the premium, owner-led positioning Blue Skies already has, but moves the estimate path higher, shortens the first step, and gives the homepage a stronger call to act.

What changes first

Lead with one clear estimate CTA instead of sending visitors deep into story content before contact.

Why this matters

The current page looks credible, but the contact step sits too low and the form asks for too much too early.

Quick fix examples

Repair the “Call of text” typo, trim the first-step form, and add service-specific CTA blocks near the top.

Reduce friction

Ask only for name, email or phone, and job type on the first step. Move preference and consent detail to step two.

Pull contact up

Bring a compact estimate box above the founder story so high-intent visitors do not have to scroll the full page first.

Clarify service fit

Use dedicated CTA cards for remodels, hot water upgrades, and new construction so the next move feels obvious.
